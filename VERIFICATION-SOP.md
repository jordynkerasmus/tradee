# Tradee — Verified Badge Review Checklist (SOP)

_A simple, repeatable process for deciding whether to grant the green **Verified**
badge. Following the same steps every time keeps it consistent and gives you a
record that you did what your FAQ says you do._

> ⚠️ Not legal advice. Have an attorney glance at this + the FAQ wording + the
> upload consent before launch (POPIA). See the legal-review task.

---

## What "Verified" means on Tradee (keep it narrow)

Granting the badge confirms **only** this:
> "This tradesman submitted identity and credential documents, and Tradee reviewed
> them and found them to be in order on their face."

It does **NOT** mean Tradee guarantees their work, their licensing is current, or
they're insured. The FAQ already says this — never claim more than the above, in
writing or verbally.

---

## Before you start
- Open **Admin → the listing's "Docs" buttons** to view the uploaded files.
- You're checking three things: **identity**, **a credential**, and **no red flags**.

## Step 1 — Identity (required)
- [ ] An SA ID / passport / driver's licence document is uploaded.
- [ ] The **name on the ID matches** the contact name / business owner on the listing.
- [ ] The photo and details are legible (not blurred, cropped or obscured).

## Step 2 — At least one credential / proof of trade (required)
Accept any **one** of:
- [ ] A trade certificate or qualification (e.g. City & Guilds, trade test, NQF cert)
- [ ] Registration with the relevant industry body (see Step 3)
- [ ] Company registration (CIPC) or a recent proof of business
- [ ] Proof of insurance (public liability)

Check it: **name matches**, **not obviously expired**, **looks genuine** (consistent
fonts, a logo/registration number, no signs of editing/tampering).

## Step 3 — Registry check for regulated trades (do this where it applies)
Many SA trades must be registered. A 1–2 minute online check adds real trust:

| Trade | Body | How to check |
|---|---|---|
| Electricians (CoC / wireman) | Dept. of Employment & Labour | Ask for their wireman's licence / registration number |
| Plumbers | **PIRB** / IOPSA | PIRB has an online registered-plumber lookup |
| Builders / home builders | **NHBRC** | NHBRC registration lookup |
| Contractors (public works) | **CIDB** | CIDB register of contractors (online) |
| Gas installers | **SAQCC Gas** | SAQCC Gas registered-practitioner search |
| Engineers | **ECSA** | ECSA registration search |

- [ ] If the trade is regulated, the registration number checks out on the body's site.
- If you **can't verify** a regulated registration, don't approve on that basis —
  approve only on identity + another credential, or ask for more.

## Step 4 — Red flags (any one = do NOT approve yet)
- ❌ Name on documents doesn't match the listing
- ❌ Document looks edited, screenshotted-and-altered, or low-quality to hide detail
- ❌ Certificate clearly expired with nothing current to replace it
- ❌ Claims a regulated registration but it doesn't appear on the official register
- ❌ Anything that feels off — when in doubt, ask, don't approve

## Step 5 — Decide (in the Admin panel)
- ✅ **Approve:** identity confirmed + at least one valid credential + no red flags
  → click **"Approve badge"**.
- 🔁 **Ask for more:** message/email them what's missing; leave unapproved until received.
- ❌ **Reject:** leave unapproved. They keep their free listing (and founding placement),
  just no badge.

## Step 6 — Keep a record
- The uploaded documents stay in the private bucket = your evidence you reviewed them.
- Optional: keep a simple log (date, who you approved, what you checked) in a sheet.

---

## Ongoing
- **Re-check on expiry:** if a credential had an expiry date, set a reminder to
  ask for a renewal; revoke the badge (toggle it off in admin) if it lapses.
- **Revoke anytime:** if you get a credible complaint or spot a problem, click the
  badge button in admin to remove it immediately.
- **Founding members:** they're featured free for 6 months, but only get the badge
  once they pass this check — so work through new uploads regularly.
